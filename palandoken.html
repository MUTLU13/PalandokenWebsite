Palandoken Skicenter Map



Palandöken is an important ski resort boasting the world's longest and steepest ski slopes. It has been ranked 18th among the top 41 ski resorts in the world by The New York Times.
The slopes in Palandöken offer excellent quality snow throughout the ski season thanks to its powder snow feature. Powder snow represents the crisp texture of fine, light, and fresh snow, providing optimal conditions for skiers. It offers a smooth surface, translating to a better skiing experience. The thin layer of snow allows for better maneuverability and fast skiing.
Various skiing and snowboarding competitions are held in different categories in Palandöken, along with snow festivals. The region boasts ten tourist facilities, four of which are 4-star and six are 5-star, along with ski houses, day facilities, and restaurants. The accommodation capacity in the ski resort covers approximately 5,530 people. These facilities offer visitors a comfortable and enjoyable winter sports experience.
Palandöken Ski Center comprises a total of 56 slopes, distributed across various difficulty levels as follows:

  • Easy Slopes: 30
  • Intermediate Slopes: 12
  • Advanced (Difficult) Slopes: 9
  • Natural Slopes: 5"
