Welcome to Palandoken


Palandöken is a significant ski resort with the world's longest and steepest ski slopes. It has been ranked 18th among the world's top 41 ski resorts by The New York Times.The slopes in Palandöken offer excellent quality snow throughout the ski season thanks to the characteristic of powder snow. Powder snow represents the crisp state of fine, light, and fresh snow. This snow structure provides the best conditions for skiers, offering a smooth surface, which means a better skiing experience. The thin layer of snow allows for better maneuverability and the opportunity for fast skiing.

Our Ski Slopes

While various skiing and snowboarding competitions are organized in Palandöken, snow festivals are also held in the region. There are ten tourist facilities in the area, four of which are 4-star and six of which are 5-star. Additionally, there are ski lodges, day facilities, and restaurants available. The accommodation capacity at the ski resort is approximately 5530 people. These amenities provide visitors with a comfortable and enjoyable winter sports experience.
Palandöken Ski Resort comprises a total of 56 slopes. These slopes vary in difficulty levels and are distributed as follows:

  • • Easy Slopes: 30
  • • Intermediate Slopes: 12
  • • Advanced (Difficult) Slopes: 9
  • • Natural Slopes: 5
Known as Palandöken2, the Konaklı Ski Resort has an additional 24.8 km of slopes. The total length of slopes in these two ski resorts amounts to 87 km. The longest ski trail is 12.5 km long, offering uninterrupted skiing throughout. Moreover, there is a vertical drop of 1100 meters between the starting and ending elevations.
There are a total of 19 lift systems at Palandöken Ski Resort, with a capacity of transporting 24,000 people per hour. Avalanche Prevention Systems: Advanced avalanche prevention systems are employed in Palandöken to reduce the risk of avalanches. These systems take preventive measures to ensure visitor safety.
Artificial Snow Systems: Artificial snow production systems are used to extend the ski season and improve snow quality (capable of producing 2600 m³ of snow per hour). This ensures that skiing conditions and the duration of the season are maintained at their best.
Slope Lighting: The slopes in Palandöken are illuminated for night skiing enthusiasts, covering a length of 9.2 km. This allows visitors to ski at any time of the day.
